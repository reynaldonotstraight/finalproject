Different Types of Cooking Saba

Banana-que

A snack made from deep-fried Saba coated in caramelized brown sugar and skewered on bamboo sticks.

Turon

A snack made of thinly sliced Saba, rolled in a spring roll wrapper, fried till the wrapper is crisp and coated with caramelized brown sugar.

Banana Chips

A snack made from Saba slices that are dried or deep-fried and can be sweet or savory.

Ginataang Saba

A traditional Filipino dessert made from boiled Saba cooked in coconut milk, sugar, and water.

Sinapot

A banana dish also known as Maruya or Baduya. The Saba are peeled and sliced and then mixed with flour, salt, baking powder, eggs and water. The mixed ingredients are then fried in boiling cooking oil.

About

Meryenda is a pan-Latin word that simply means morning or afternoon snack, but it is always more satisfying than that, just like of how you make a Meryenda with Saba. Meryenda is that sense of comfort, satisfaction, and kinship. Our goal is to give you an ideas of how to cook Saba in a different ways of it.
